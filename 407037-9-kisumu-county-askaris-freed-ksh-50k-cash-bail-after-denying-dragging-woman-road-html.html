9 Kisumu County Askaris Freed on KSh 50k cash Bail After Denying Dragging Woman on Road

- The county enforcement officers were produced before a Kisumu Law Courts Chief Magistrate Martha Agutu on Friday, March 26

- The court was told that the nine allegedly dragged Beatrice Atieno from a speeding vehicle within the township in Kisumu central sub-county

- The victim was represented by lawyers from the Federation of Women Lawyers, Law Society of Kenya and the Kenya Human Rights Commission

Kisumu nine askaris who were caught on camera dragging a female hawker on the tarmacked road have pleaded not guilty causing grievous bodily harm to her.

The nine suspects were Victor Odiyo Ochieng, Kevin Omondi, George Nyonje, Faith Akoth, Paul Ochieng, Martin Olwa, Japheth Odero, Vincent Okeyo and Josephat Rowa.

The county enforcement officers were produced before a Kisumu Law Courts Chief Magistrate Martha Agutu on Friday, March 26, after spending the night at the police custody.

The court was told that the nine allegedly dragged Beatrice Atieno from a speeding vehicle within the township in Kisumu central sub-county jointly with three others, not before the court.

In a video that circulated online on Wednesday, March 24, a white Pickup truck was filmed speeding through the streets as the hopeless trader screamed from behind.

Atieno was represented by lawyers Jackeline Ingutia, Onsongo and Joshua Nyamori from the Federation of Women Lawyers-Kisumu chapter, Law Society of Kenya and the Kenya Human Rights Commission.

The lawyers said they had received information indicating the accused had threatened their client after the incident was highlighted.

On their part, the defense advocates Gerald Kadede and Mariela Awuor, urged the court to be lenient on the county employees saying the suspects were the sole breadwinners.

“I, therefore, pray that you release them on reasonable bond terms and ask that we be supplied with the copies of the charge sheet and witnesses’ statement,” said Kadede.

The magistrate freed each suspect on KSh 50,000 cash bail or KSh 80,000 Sh50,000 bond.

The case will be mentioned on April 13 and heard on April 20.

Suspension

Following the incident, Kisumu governor Anyang' Nyong'o suspended the askaris to pave way for investigation.

The county boss said he was away on official duties in Nairobi when the unfortunate incident occurred.

Nyong'o stated he was saddened and shocked by the ugly ordeal and rebuked his officers for their actions.

"While away in Nairobi on official Council of Governors’ meetings, I have been saddened and shocked by a regrettable incident in Kisumu, following the arrest and mishandling of a lady trader by the city's enforcement officers on Wednesday, March 24, morning," Nyong'o said.

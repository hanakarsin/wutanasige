Lee Funeral Home: Trending Photos Were Taken During Ex-President Daniel Moi's Death

- A section of Kenyans on Twitter have been spreading fear and tension using old photos of Lee Funeral Home for the better part of Friday, March 19

- The photos being shared were taken in 2020 during the death of late former president Daniel Moi

- A representative from Lee Funeral Home also spoke exclusively to TUKO.co.ke and confirmed there was no VVIP in the morgue

On the morning of Friday, March 19, a section of Kenyans on Twitter shared pictures of Lee Funeral Home that ostensibly caused fear and anxiety.

In the photos that were shared, a contingent of officers from the Kenya Defence Forces (KDF) was seen outside the funeral home which is associated with the preservation of the remains of the high and mighty in Kenya.

Speculations started running wild on social media with many falsely assuring Kenyans to wait for some "big news".

However, TUKO.co.ke can authoritatively confirm the pictures being used to spread tension on social media are actually old and were taken in 2020 when former president Daniel Arap Moi died.

TUKO.co.ke's cameraman Bebeto Ogutu shot the photos in February 2020 when the military took over at the high-end morgue to guard Moi's body.

A representative from Lee Funeral Home also spoke exclusively to TUKO.co.ke and confirmed the pictures circulating on social media are just spreading unnecessary worry as they do not represent the exact situation on the ground.

"Those pictures were taken during ex-president Daniel Moi's death. We do not have a VVIP in the home; those are just rumours," he confirmed.

Perhaps, many Kenyans were too quick to fall for the rumours as they emerged just a day after the world woke up to the news of Tanzania's John Magufuli's death.

The Tanzanian leader passed away on Wednesday, March 17, at 6pm, according to an announcement made by Vice President Samia Suluhu on state television.

According to Suluhu, Magufuli began feeling unwell and was taken to Jakaya Kikwete Heart Institute on Saturday, March 6, and though he was discharged days later, his situation got worse on Sunday, March 14.

Suluhu has since been sworn-in as the sixth Tanzanian president and she also announced that the late head of state will be buried in Chato District on March 25 as per his wishes.

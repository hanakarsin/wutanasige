Jubilee Party has suffered a major blow in its quest to expel six nominated senators allied to Deputy President William Ruto.

The ruling outfit had shown the door nominated senators Millicent Omanga, Isaac Mwaura, Seneta Mary Yiane, Waqo Naomi Jilo, Prengei Victor and Iman Falhada Dekow over disciplinary violations.

However, on Wednesday, May 5, the Political Parties Dispute Tribunal (PPDT) chaired by Desma Nungo declared the expulsion illegal.

"A declaration is hereby issued that the decision to expel the applicants was unlawful and against the Jubilee Constitution," said Nungo as quoted by Standard.

The tribunal argued that the ruling party did not follow its constitution while expelling the six lawmakers.

The tribunal said that the legislators were not summoned but instead appeared before the disciplinary committee, thinking that the complaints against them had been dropped.

The tribunal consequently ruled it was unfair for the party not to give the senators a chance to counter the claims against them effectively.

However, the six are not out of the woods yet as the tribunal directed that Jubilee can still initiate a fresh disciplinary process but follow the law.

According to the outfit's secretary-general, Raphael Tuju, the National Management Committee (NMC) met on Monday, February 8, and resolved to expel the lawmakers on grounds of disciplinary violations and gross misconduct.

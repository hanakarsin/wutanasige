Ngong: 10 KDF Soldiers Die in Crash, 13 Hospitalised at Defence Memorial Hospital

  • Kajiado deputy county commissioner Morang'a Morekwa said 13 other soldiers sustained serious injuries
  • The Mi-171E model helicopter was on a training mission when it came down at Ol Tepesi area on Thursday morning
  • The KDF spokesperson Zipporah Kioko indicated all the passengers had been accounted for and moved to safety

At least 10 Kenya Defence Forces (KDF) soldiers have perished in the early morning chopper crash in Ngong, Kajiado county.

Kajiado deputy county commissioner Morang'a Morekwa said 13 other passengers sustained serious injuries and were rushed to Defence Forces Memorial Hospital, Nairobi.

The Mi-171E model helicopter was on a training mission when it came down at Ol Tepesi area at around 9 am on Thursday, June 24, according to KDF.

Witnesses said it circled in the air before it crash-landed, after which it caught fire.

“Today at around 0900hrs, an Mi 171e Air Force Helicopter, on a training mission crash-landed at the OlTepesi area in Ngong, Kaiiado County,” said KDF in an earlier statement.

The KDF spokesperson Zipporah Kioko indicated all the passengers had been accounted for and moved to safety.

"Regrettably, following the crash, we suffered fatalities. The process of communicating with the families to relay the information and KDF condolences is underway," Kioko stated.

She added that aircraft accident investigators were also at the crash site to establish the cause of the crash.

Mi-171E features

Mi-171E is an advanced, multi-purpose helicopter produced by Ulan-Ude Aviation Plant, a part of Russian Helicopters.

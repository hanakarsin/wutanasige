Emmah Shisoka: Family of Atheists in Kenya Society's Secretary Says Daughter's Decision Surprised Th

- The Atheists in Kenya Society had announced that Seth Mahiga stepped aside as the association's secretary after he found Jesus

- The society thereafter unveiled Emmah Shisoka as its new secretary

- Shisoka's family was, however, baffled by the news, given their daughter had grown up in a Christian family

The family of Emmah Shisoka says their daughter's decision to join the Atheists In Kenya Society got them off guard and was not an easy one to take.

Shisoka took over as the new secretary of the society following Seth Mahiga's resignation.

Armed with a Journalism and Mass Communication degree from Masinde Muliro University of Science and Technology (MMUST), Shisoka replaced Mahiga as the association's secretary after he resigned because of finding Jesus, whom he spent years denying his existence.

Sources close to Shisoka's family whispered to TUKO.co.ke that Emmah's parents never expected their daughter, raised in a Christian family, would make such a decision because she had never mentioned such.

"The father told me that he was perplexed to hear the direction the daughter had taken. He said it was difficult to explain to anyone who inquired about his girl's decision. As a father he said they had taken the new development the way it had come but were optimistic things would get better," a source close to the family said.

According to the source, Emmah's father, who currently works in Bungoma, disclosed that their daughter had notably begun to distance herself from them and faith matters.

However, her diverting into atheism was not something they had expected as a family.

"He was heard saying, somehow the move was a tough one for them but adults are at liberty to make decisions. Though he insisted that being an adult does not stop one from being someone's child and as parents they had little to say, at least for the moment they are absorbing the news," added the source.

The source who sought anonymity said that the atheists' secretary's dad is a strong believer in Christ and regularly praised and attributed his survival from a near-fatal accident to God's wondrous deeds.

"Those who know the dad can tell you how he praises God for saving him from an accident, helping him out of a wheelchair and making him throw away crutches," she added.

Shisoka's predecessor stepped down from the position, citing he had found Jesus Christ and is no longer interested in promoting atheism in Kenya.

In a statement on Sunday, May 30, the society's president Harrison Mumia eulogised him as a dedicated leader, wishing him the best in his newfound relationship with Jesus Christ.

"We wish Seth all the best in his newfound relationship with Jesus Christ, we thank him for having served the society with dedication over the last one and half years," partly read the society's statement.

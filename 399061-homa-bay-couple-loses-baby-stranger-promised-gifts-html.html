Homa Bay couple loses baby to stranger who promised them gifts

- Benta Akinyi met a woman who became a friend during her final days of pregnancy at a local hospital in Homa Bay county

- They exchange contacts with the new friend promising to buy presents for Benta's baby once she delivered

- On Wednesday, January 5, Benta agreed to meet with her friend so she could receive the present

- Upon meeting, the suspect duped the mother of one that the present was with the other woman who was on the way coming

- In the process, Benta asked her 'friend' to hold the baby so she could make her hair at a nearby saloon but the woman disappeared with the child after a short while

A young couple from Masakla village, Kanyach Kachar location in Homa Bay county is in agony after their baby was stolen by a stranger who promised them gifts.

Benta Akinyi, 21, and her husband Austine Odhiambo, 27, are now looking for the whereabouts of their first-born child who was stolen by an unfamiliar woman in Rodi Kopany township.

Fake friendship

The infant named Stanley Desmond Odhiambo was stolen by the woman who duped the mother by faking friendship.

According to Benta, she was admitted to one of the local hospitals during her pregnancy where she met the suspect and they exchanged contacts.

"After that day the woman was very concerned about my well being and she used to call me asking when I'll give birth," Benta said.

After giving birth, the said woman called Benta and told her to let her know when she would be taking the baby to the clinic, claiming she had some presents that she wanted to give the baby.

"I told her that I will be taking the baby to the clinic after two weeks, that is when she asked me where to meet her so that she could hand over the gifts to me," Benta said.

Meeting at Rodi Legio Maria church

The mother of one said the suspect requested to meet her with the child near Rodi Legio Maria church on the evening of Tuesday, January 5, so she could gift the baby a present.

Upon meeting and exchanging pleasantries, the suspect told her the gifts were with another woman who was on the way coming.

"By that time, my hair was shaggy so I told her to escort me to the salon, she agreed and even carried the baby for me," she said

While at the salon the woman told Benta the smoke from the blow-dry was not good for the baby's health and moved outside the salon with the infant.

A few minutes later, the lady disappeared with the baby and switched off her phone.

Appeal for help

The couple is now appealing to the police and area residents to help them trace their son.

Odhiambo said the baby was their first born and they did not have any other child.

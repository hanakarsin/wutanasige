Murang'a: Mukurwe wa Nyagathanga Shrine to Undergo Cleansing after Justin Muturi's Coronation

- The elders under the Kikuyu Council of Elders Association said that the ceremony would take place tomorrow, Friday, June 4

- Mugo Njoroge, one of the organisers of the event, said that Speaker Justin Muturi's coronation informed the cleansing on Saturday, May 22

- Murangá governor Mwangi Wa Iria downplayed the crowning of Justine Muturi, saying the shrine is not meant for coronating leaders but traditional prayers

A group of elders from Murangá county has said that preparations to cleanse Mukurwe wa Nyagathanga shrine have been concluded.

The elders under the Kikuyu Council of Elders Association said that the ceremony would occur tomorrow, Friday, June 4.

"We shall conduct a cleansing ceremony at the Mukuruwe wa Nyagathanga at Gaturi where our leaders popularly known as high priests or cultural priests from the Kikuyu community will conduct the exercise," said Mugo Njoroge, one of the organisers of the event.

Justine Muturi's coronation

He said that the cleansing was informed by Speaker Justin Muturi's coronation on Saturday, May 22.

According to the event organisers, the occasion turned political, and the presence of police officers at the facility was an abuse to the shrine.

"There are some people who have been asking us why we are doing this. Some people recently entered the shrine, yet they are not permitted there. They entered the shrine with weapons and shoes. This is why our elders will be going there to pray to God to forgive all of us who might have gone against our traditions," Njoroge added.

Njoroge said that the event ceremony would be presided over by leaders from Murang’a county.

"We have also assured our security officers that it is our responsibility to ensure there is peace during the entire exercise. Our elders are peace-loving people who dint engage in politics. Unfortunately, some people want to bring politicise the shrine," he said.

COVID-19 health protocols

Another event organiser Peter Munga said that the police had initially tried to block the event, but the issue has since been sorted out.

"We only had one challenge when we informed the police about our meeting, and they wrote to us a letter denying us the opportunity to hold the meeting. They were worried about our security and the COVID-19 health protocols. We told them that we had found a bigger venue at Mumbi Stadium where we shall sit down per the COVID-19 rules as our elders continue with the ceremony on the other side, and they agreed to the request," Munga explained.

Munga further assured the elders of going ahead with the ceremony, assuring them of their security.

"For a very long time, there has been unnecessary antagonism between the Kikuyu Council of Elders and Kiama Kiama, but we had already made efforts, come together, and we are acting as one about giving the Kikuyu nation clear and direct guidance," said Munga.

Another event organiser Mukuna was Kariuki, who said that there is no division in this particular case.

"It is only that some people are not happy when Kikuyus are united, but we are going to assure them that we are united more than ever. Whatever happened at the shrine on May 22 was very unfortunate because it was a sign of commercializing elderhood," Kariuki explained.

A contingent of police officers

Muranga governor Mwangi Wa Iria said that they don't expect a contingent of police officers at the venue of the cleansing ceremony.

"This is not a day of going to war, so we don’t want to see guns there. All we want to see are the flywhisks. We also don’t want to see blue uniforms because our elders will be putting on their brown attire and ceremonial hats," said Wa Iria.

KCSE: George Magoha Warns There Are Fake Exam Papers Circulating In Market

- George Magoha confirmed the confiscation of eight mobile phones from candidates across the country

- The education cabinet secretary said the examination has been credibly fair save for few cases reported here and there

- A KCSE invigilator was arrested in Migori county by education officials for engaging in exams irregularity

The Ministry of Education has admitted few cases of examination malpractices that have so far been witnessed in the ongoing Kenya Certificate of Secondary Education (KCSE).

Education Cabinet Secretary George Magoha said although the examination had been credibly fair, he warned there were some fake examination papers in circulation.

"The new thing is that there people who have started to sell fake papers. I, therefore, warn parents and teachers not to be duped into buying or even utilising the question papers," he said.

Speaking on day four of KCSE at Upper Hill High School on Thursday, March 1, Magoha confirmed the confiscation of eight mobile phones from candidates across the country.

"Eight candidates were found with mobile phones in the examination rooms. These cellphones were confiscated from various examination centres for investigations. The examinations have been very credible despite a few incidents and attempts to cheat all of which have been nipped in the bud," he added.

On Tuesday, March 30, an invigilator was arrested in Migori county by education officials for engaging in exam irregularity.

The suspect and three other teachers, including a school principal, were charged at Rongo Law Courts.

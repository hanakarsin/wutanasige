North Eastern leaders call for arrest of Governor Ngilu over incitement remarks

- Governor Ngilu was accused of inciting Kitui locals against neighbouring Somali community

- The sentiments irated Northerners who called for press conference on the matter

- They called on Interior CS to ensure the county boss is arrested and prosecuted immediately

A section of Members of Parliament from North Eastern have called for arrest of Kitui Governor Charity Ngilu over alleged anti-Somali remarks captured in an audio clip that has gone viral.

Speaking to Journalist at Parliament buildings on Wednesday, September 25, the Northerners led by Mandera North MP Bashir Abdullahi accused Ngilu of profiling their community hence necessitating their call for her immediate arrest and prosecution.

“I want to tell Kenyans utterances made by Ngilu are as a result of her ailing political career. I am sure that is not the position of our neighbours in Kitui county because we have lived in harmony since time immemorial,” Bashir said

He further called on Interior Cabinet Secretary Fred Matiang'i to stop forthwith repatriation of nomads from Kitui county.

The legislator said, only individuals with illegal firearms were the ones who needed to be dealt with.

His sentiments were echoed by Aldas MP Aden Keynan who urged political leaders to refrain from uttering incitement words saying such reckless actions would destroy the long standing relationship the two communities have been enjoying for decades.

“Kenya is not a banana republic, we are governed by the rule of law and we demand the government of the day to arrest and prosecute Ngilu otherwise we shall feel sidelined,” Keynan noted.

On the question why Somali community have been marauding with firearms, Bura MP Ali Wario said every community in every part of the country have had few elements of crime amon.

He said such individuals needed to be dealt with individually as crime perpetrators and not as a reason to punish entire community.

“This nonsense of saying these are from this community is unfortunate. We shouldn’t start calling out our names by the tribe. Let us look for amicable solutions from our systems,” Wajir West MP Ahmed Kolosh added.

However, National Cohesion and Integration Commission (NCIC) said the viral audio clip was captured an incident that had happened in 2018.

According to the commission, the matter was on the final stages of their assessment.

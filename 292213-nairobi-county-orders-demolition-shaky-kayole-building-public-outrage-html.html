Nairobi County orders for demolition of shaky Kayole building after public outrage

The county government of Nairobi has ordered that a seven-storey building being erected along Kayole Spine Road be pulled down.

This came after pictures of the visibly faulty structure were shared widely on social media and thus triggered public outrage.

On Friday, November 9, the county had directed the owner of the building to bring it down but the developer did not comply.

"You are hereby required to stop further construction immediately and submit architectural and structure plan," the notice read in part.

The notice expired on Wednesday, November 16, and among those who reacted on social media wondered why the dangerous house had not been destroyed.

Others wondered how the structure went through the approval stages by respective authorities.

Demolition of dangerous structures and those constructed on public land was started by the government and has since seen destruction of several high-end buildings.

Among buildings affected by the move were South End Mall along Lang'ata road, Ukay Center, Airgate Center (Taj Mall) and a section of Westland's Oshwal Center.

A petrol station and a Java restaurant in Kileleshwa were also destroyed in the initial stages of the crackdown.

Another building currently being targeted is the Seefar Apartment which National Environment and Management Authority (NEEMA) demands be pulled down by February 2019.

The building, according, to NEEMA has been constructed on public land.

9 Barcelona Senior Players Who Could Be Sold This Summer

- Barcelona have been tipped to undertake a radical surgery on their squad ahead of the summer window

- The Catalans boss Ronald Koeman is looking to rebuild a fresh team that can challenge for titles next campaign

- Coutinho, Dembele, and seven others have already been earmarked for sale to give room for fresh legs at the club

Barcelona boss Ronald Koeman is reportedly getting ready to offload nine players when the summer transfer window opens.

The Dutchman was named the Catalans boss last August and he immediately got rid of a number of players including Luis Suarez, Ivan Rakitic and Nelson Semedo who he considered surplus to requirement.

Sources claim the 58-year-old is still not satisfied with his current and will further trim it down to give space for new additions at the end of this season.

After his arrival last year, Koeman was said to have pointed to the players he wants the club to sign but they could not due to the financial crisis caused by COVID-19.

The trio of Memphis Depay, Georginio Wijnaldum, and Eric Garcia were top on the manager's wishlist but could not get any of them immediately due to cash crunch as reported by The Mirror.

Barca have also been linked with a move for out-of-contract Manchester City legend Sergio Aguero who shares the national team dressing room with Lionel Messi in their country.

The Camp Nou outfit has suffered terrible losses in recent times mostly in the Champions League while they failed to win any silverware last term.

Although the Blaugrana have crashed out of the Champions League, they are just one point behind Atletico Madrid on the La Liga table after 29 games this campaign.

And to ensure he further strengthens his squad to challenge for more titles, Koeman is said to have identified players he wants moved on.

Philippe Coutinho, Martin Braithwaite and defender Samuel Umtiti headline a list of nine senior players who are likely to be offloaded.

Others are Ousmane Dembele, Miralem Pjanic, Sergi Roberto, Junior Firpo, Carles Alena and Matheus Fernandes.

Alena has been out on loan at Getafe this season and both clubs have already held discussions over completing a permanent deal in the summer.

Meanwhile, Fernandes has stuck around under Koeman's watch but has played just once - as a 73rd-minute substitute against Dynamo Kyiv - while Ilaix Moriba seems to have overtaken him in the pecking order.

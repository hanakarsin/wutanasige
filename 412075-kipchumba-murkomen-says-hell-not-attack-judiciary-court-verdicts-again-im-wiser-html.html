Kipchumba Murkomen Says He'll Not Attack Judiciary over Court Verdicts Again: &quot;I'm Wiser&quot;

Elgeyo Marakwet senator Kipchumba Murkomen has admitted to his past mistake of attacking the Supreme Court judges for nullifying the 2017 presidential election.

The former Senate majority leader said at no one point will he ever blast the Judiciary again for the decision taken by the court.

"You remind me of the things we said about the Supreme Court after the nullification of Uhuru’s elections. Now I am wiser. Good luck to ODM, NASA, and Kieleweke as you attack the Judiciary. I am never going to be a party to such madness anymore," he stated.

Murkomen was reacting to the Building Bridges Initiative bill co-drafter lawyer Paul Mwangi who sneered at the High Court judges for declaring the process null and void.

In a series of tweets, Mwangi claimed the judges hijacked the petitioners' case and used it to wage war on President Uhuru Kenyatta instead of dealing with the issues that had been raised.

"Yesterday's BBI judgment was not about protecting a constitution. It was about destroying a country," he claimed.

In 2017, Murkomen and other Jubilee Party politicians took a swipe at the former chief justice David Maraga for overturning President Uhuru Kenyatta's victory.

Maraga had led a six-judge bench that nullified the election of President Uhuru Kenyatta, citing illegalities and lack of consistency with the constitution.

It is the Kenyan people that are supreme," Deputy President William Ruto said then.

Madaraka Day: Woman Rep Esther Passaris takes another swipe at Governor Mike Sonko

- Passaris demanded women must be respected not just at public functions but everywhere else including in matatus

- It is understood she was reacting to how the Nairobi governor treated her during the Madaraka Day celebrations

- Passaris walked out on the governor after he embarrassed her in front of a crowd for poking holes into his leadership style

A day after a dramatic public spat with Governor Mike Mbuvi Sonko, Nairobi woman representative Esther Passaris has come out to demand respect for women.

Seemingly still angry at Sonko over how he treated her during the Madaraka Day celebrations on Saturday, June 1, Passaris took another swipe at the governor saying women must be respected not just at public functions but everywhere else.

The woman rep disclosed she was part of the president's delegation enroute to Women Deliver 2019 Global Conference to be held in Canada on Monday, June 3, and that she was overwhelmed by the love and support she was receiving from Kenya and beyond.

" I just landed at Schiphol enroute to Women Deliver as part of the president's delegation and I am overwhelmed by the love and support from Kenya and across the world. Women must be respected; in boardrooms, in matatus and in public functions. My service is to the People of Nairobi," she tweeted on Sunday morning.

READ ALSO: Madaraka Day: Esther Passaris walks out of dais after Sonko mentions her husband

Passaris' remark was understood to be a reaction to an embarrassing public dress down she received from Governor Sonko a day earlier when the two leaders met at Pumwani police grounds in Nairobi to celebrate Kenya's independence day.

Trouble started when Passaris while delivering her Madaraka Day speech accused Sonko of sidelining other leaders in the running of Nairobi county affairs.

The ODM party politician claimed the governor often snubbed her phone calls regardless of urgency of the matter at hand, allegations that the county boss did not take kindly.

In his response, Sonko told off the woman rep for demanding that he picks her calls saying he is not her husband.

"I do not pick up calls when I am in a meeting like this one. Sometimes I ring president but he does not return my calls. I am not your husband to receive your phone calls whenever you want," the governor said, prompting Passaris to walk out on him.

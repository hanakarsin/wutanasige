Ruto's Allies Come Clear on Plot to Impeach Uhuru after BBI Court Verdict: &quot;We've No Intentions&quot;

Allies of Deputy President William Ruto have downplayed claims they are planning to initiate an impeachment process against President Uhuru Kenyatta.

The leaders allied to the United Democratic Alliance (UDA) said they are not interested in initiating an impeachment process despite the Thursday, May 13, court verdict against the president's backed constitutional amendment initiative.

While upholding a consolidated case by eight petitioners, they said the 14-member Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) steering committee, which was gazetted by Uhuru, and led by the late Garissa Senator Yusuf Haji, was unconstitutional.

The petition to stop the amendment to the 2010 Constitution was filed by several like-minded Kenyans who stated they would like to protect it from erosion.

The bench, led by Justice Joel Ngugi, said Uhuru blundered in initiating a process to change the constitution through a popular initiative.

The court also observed that Uhuru was in breach of the constitution, an impeachable offence.

Led by MPs Alice Wahome (Kandara) and Sylvanus Osoro (South Mugirango), Ruto's allies termed the judgment a boast for the constitution and sovereignty of the country.

“We celebrate the judiciary. They have redeemed the nation from the shackles of dictatorship. You cannot make the constitution-making process your own bid. The document belongs to Kenyans. Ordinary Kenyans have been talking about all these issues,” said Wahome.

Osoro condemned individuals who allegedly misled Uhuru in initiating the BBI process.

“It is on record that as parliamentarians, we wanted to know who gave Uhuru powers to initiate such a process, “said Osoro.

Osoro said Uhuru should explain how monies spent on the BBI process will be recovered and outline how the court’s concerns shall be resolved.

He also laid the blame on the country’s attorney general, Kihara Kariuki.

"The brave judges have given this country time to celebrate. This is a jurisprudence across the world. Give people options on what they want to change,” added Osoro.

TUKO.co.ke had reported that behind the petitions to stop the BBI process was a list of seasoned lawyers who successfully argued their case before a five-judge bench.

Some of the lawyers were Nelson Havi, Waikwa Wanyoike, Korir Sing'oei, Esther Ang'awa, and Charles Kanjama.

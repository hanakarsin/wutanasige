Orie Rogo Manduli: 7 Powerful Photos of Unforgettable Flamboyant, Bold Politician

  • Orie Rogo Manduli was the first woman of African descent to participate in the East African Safari Rally in 1974 and 1975
  • The Manduli was known for her signature vitenge outfits and uniquely tied headgears which matched her attires
  • Manduli was a teacher by training but never stepped into a class to teach

Kenyans were on the evening of Wednesday, September 8, sent into mourning following the sudden demise of legendary and stylish female politician Orie Rogo Manduli.

The news of her death was confirmed by her family who said that the renowned politician died at her home in Nairobi.

However, it is still unclear what led to the politician's death.

TUKO.co.ke highlights some of her unforgettable moments during her lifetime.

1. First ever Kenyan woman rally driver

Before joining politics, late Orie Rogo Manduli had made a name for herself in the safari rally field.

TUKO.co.ke understands late Orie was the first woman of African descent to participate in the East African Safari Rally in 1974 and 1975.

2. Unrivaled fashion sense

Being a lady from the lakeside, late Orie Rogo Manduli was blessed with an impressive sense of style.

The female politician used to rock bespoke kitenge outfits which were somewhat her signature style.

3. Unique headgears

Manduli is one of the few Kenyan women who loved headgear or rather turbans.

The late Manduli used to step out rocking huge headgear which had been tied in very unique styles.

4. Fearless Activist

Manduli, having been in politics for a while and among the few female politicians, she was at the forefront in championing the rights of women and children.

5. Distinguished leader

Late Orie Rogo was once the International Council of Women’s permanent representative to the United Nations Environment Programme and Habitat, a role she dealt with in the best way possible.

6. Teacher by training

Manduli went to the Machakos Teachers College and graduated.

However, the late Manduli never stepped into class to teach after graduation as she picked a different interest.

7. English enthusiast

Manduli used to captivate her followers and Kenyans in general with her knowledge of the English language.

She was interesting to listen to in interviews and even in public forums such as rallies.

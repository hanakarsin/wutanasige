Preparations underway for late Kenneth Matiba's cremation

- Preparations to have Matiba's body cremated at Lanag'ata crematorium have started

- The late veteran politician had indicated he did not want his body to be buried

- His family chose to honour his wishes despite government having offered to accord him a State burial

- Matiba died at the age of 85 after ailing for a long time

The best send off a family would give a fallen hero is honouring their wish.

So did the family of the late veteran politician and father democracy and second liberation icon, Kenneth Matiba.

Matiba's greatest desire was to have his body cremated as he did not fancy a pompous burial like many Kenyans had expected.

After spending hours behind closed doors pondering on the same, his family agreed to have his body cremated evena after residents of Kimabu and Murang'a counties tussled over where he should be laid to rest.

Information gathered by TUKO.co.ke indicated the family settled on having Matiba's body cremated at Lang’ata crematorium in Nairobi.

Matiba died on Sunday, April 15, at the Karen Hospital, Nairobi, where he was undergoing treatment after ailing for years.

A second funeral service was held on Thursday, April 26, at his home in Murang'a after the first one on Wednesday, April 25, at the All Saints’ Cathedral, Nairobi.

Matiba will be remembered for his selfless and painful struggles, including detention without trial and torture during the KANU regime under retired President Daniel Moi.

He will also be remembered for his spirited fight for multi-party democracy that Kenya enjoys today.

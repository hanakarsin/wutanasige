Woman who gave birth at Uhuru Park appeals for job, scholarship to raise child

- Rebecca gave birth at Uhuru Park after being kicked out by her landlord

- The 20-year-old is looking for a job or scholarship to be able to cater for herself

- The companies that offered to pay her school fees and giver her a job have since defaulted

A homeless woman who delivered a baby at Uhuru Park on Wednesday, March 13, after being kicked out by her landlord is appealing for a job.

20-year-old Rebecca Atieno is now looking for a scholarship or someone to give her a job after two companies that had promised to give her a job and pay her school fees defaulted.

Wanjiru Kihusa, a maternal health advocate, went on Twitter to inform the public of the latest on the young mother.

READ ALSO: Diana Marua narrates how singer Weezdom turned against her despite not knowing her

She(Kihusa) revealed that Rebecca needed to continue with the next phase of her life and after being housed by a good Samaritan by the name Christine for the past eight months.

Christine, however, doesn't want Rebecca to move without having a solid base for her to stay.

Rebecca desires either to go back to school or get a job as she only got to class eight in her educational journey.

A sum of Ksh 74,457 was donated by well-wishers and Kihusa says it will be used in the following areas:

  • Ksh. 6000 to pay NHIF for Rebecca and daughter Hope for one year.
  • Another sum to move and settle her into her own place.
  • Another sum to be saved in an account of her choice.
    • "This money would be more useful if we had a specific next step. If you can offer this lady a job or a scholarship to school please reach out. She only has a class 8 certificate so I know that limits choices but she's passionate about beauty and hospitality," Wanjiru Kihusa wrote on her Twitter.

    The 20-year-old mum is said to be awaiting her National identity card which was applied in August.

    In March, St Johns Ambulance came to Rebecca's help after her water broke at Uhuru Park helping her to safe birth and transporting her to the hospital.

    Atieno had earlier lost a job at a hotel where she used to work. Her landlord in Muthurwa had evicted her after she couldnt afford to pay KSh 50, which was the daily fee.

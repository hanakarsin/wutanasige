Yoruba Tradition: How Ooni Of Ife's Death Should Be Announced

published on March 01, 2024
Yoruba people have been one of the most dominant groups in the country with a very rich culture.  The religion of the people comprise of traditional religious and spiritual concepts and practices of the Yoruba people. The Yoruba's are realistic and pragmatic about their attitude to death.  In Yoruba belief, death is not the end of life; rather, it is a transition from one form of existence to another. There is a belief in an afterlife that is a continuation of this life, only in a different setting, and the abode of the dead is usually placed at a place just outside of this abode, and is sometimes thought of as separated by a stream.
